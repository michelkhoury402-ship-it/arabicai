Internal Strategy Memo · March 2026

We're running a peacetime org in a wartime market.

A proposal to restructure Arabic.ai's go to market around vertical pods. Replace the territory model with cross-functional attack teams built for speed, depth, and capital efficiency.

01 · The Diagnosis

Our GTM model was built for a different company.

We adopted a flat, territory based sales org. Each rep owns a geography and covers all of Arabic.ai's product surface. This is the big tech playbook. It works at scale with mature products and established product market fit.

We are not big tech. We are a startup trying to find product market fit in a rapidly shifting market. War started in MENA, our primary region. Investors are applying pressure. The window to prove capital efficiency is shrinking every month.

The result: everyone goes wide, nobody goes deep. Reps context switch across industries every call. Knowledge doesn't compound. Pipeline is broad but shallow. Deals stall in procurement. Product builds features nobody asked for because there's no tight feedback loop from the market.

3 to 6mo Average sales cycle for enterprise deals
~15% Estimated win rate on qualified opportunities
0 Reps with deep vertical expertise in any single domain
Low Pipeline visibility. Each territory is a black box.
02 · The Thesis

Stop covering territories.
Start attacking verticals.

Reorganize the company around small, cross-functional pods. Each one laser focused on one vertical or product motion. Each pod has its own sales, marketing, product, and engineering. Each pod operates like a startup within the startup.

This is how Palantir went from $0 to $1B+. Small forward deployed teams that go absurdly deep, win a lighthouse customer, build a reference, and expand. It's how Anduril won defence contracts. It's the proven playbook for early stage AI companies selling into complex verticals.

A pod doesn't just sell. It builds, learns, and iterates every week. The feedback loop between "what the customer needs" and "what we ship" shrinks from months to days.

03 · The Culture Shift

Everyone sells. No exceptions.

In a startup at our stage, selling is not a department. It is the company's primary function. Every single person at Arabic.ai needs to be generating pipeline, building relationships, and driving revenue. Full stop.

Product managers should be on customer calls daily, not writing specs in isolation. Engineers should be demoing prototypes to prospects, not waiting for tickets. Marketing should be creating assets that directly generate meetings, not brand awareness campaigns. Partnership people should be co-selling, not building decks about "ecosystem strategy."

If you work at Arabic.ai today, your job is to help close deals. Your title is secondary. Your quota contribution is primary. This is what it means to operate like a startup.

This isn't about turning everyone into cold callers. It's about orienting the entire company around revenue. A product manager who sits in on a sales call and hears the objection firsthand will build a better product than one who reads a summary in a Slack thread. An engineer who demos their own work to a prospect will ship faster than one who never meets a customer.

06 · The Deal Size Shift

Smaller entry. Faster close. Bigger expansion.

Our current approach enters high with $200K+ proposals that trigger procurement reviews, legal cycles, and committee approvals. In a wartime market, this is too slow.

The play: land with a $30 to 80K pilot that sits below procurement thresholds. Prove value in 4 to 6 weeks. Then expand into a six figure contract as a renewal conversation, not a new sale.

$50K Average pilot size. Closes in weeks, not months.
4 to 6wk Pilot delivery timeline. Fast enough to prove value.
3x Faster reference customer acquisition
$200K+ Expansion target after successful pilot

More at bats means more data means faster product market fit. Every pilot teaches us something. Every churned pilot teaches us more.

07 · Lead Management

Tighter routing. Full visibility. No black boxes.

In the current model, leads are assigned by territory. A senior rep and a junior rep get the same quality leads based on geography. Warm midbound leads sometimes sit untouched because the territory owner is busy elsewhere.

In the pod model, lead routing is intentional:

  • 1 Midbound and warm leads go to senior reps with domain expertise. The people most likely to convert them.
  • 2 Cold outbound is run by dedicated outbound reps doing 80+ touches per week. Not senior closers.
  • 3 Daily pod standups. Who is calling who today. What is the blocker. What advanced.
  • 4 Weekly pipeline review with CEO visibility. Every deal tracked. Every stall surfaced.
  • 5 Kill slow deals fast. If a prospect won't commit to a pilot within 4 weeks, move on. We cannot afford long cycles.

09 · The 90 Day Pilot

Three pods. 90 days. Let the data decide.

We're not proposing a theoretical reorg. We're proposing a controlled experiment with three parallel teams and clear success criteria.

Weeks 1 to 2

Stand Up All Three Pods

Reassign existing people into pods. No new hires. Each pod defines its ICP, target accounts, outreach playbook, and first MVP scope. ContentLab pod scopes its agent architecture and identifies first human in the loop workflows.

Weeks 3 to 8

Attack

All pods run full cycle in parallel. Outbound blitz on banking and defence. ContentLab ships first working agent with human review loop. 3 to 5 pilot proposals out per vertical pod. Marketing ships vertical specific assets for each pod.

Weeks 8 to 12

Evaluate

Compare pod metrics across all three. Qualified pipeline generated. Time from first contact to signed pilot. Revenue closed. Customer engagement depth. Which pod has the strongest signal?

Week 12+

Double Down

Scale the pods that work. Restructure the rest of the org around the winning motions. Hire into high performing pods with conviction and budget.

10 · The Ask

Greenlight three pods. 90 days. Launch Monday.

This isn't a restructuring proposal. It's three parallel bets with high signal. We reassign existing team members. No new hires. No new budget. We set clear metrics upfront and let the data tell us which motions to scale.

The cost of trying this is 90 days of focused effort. The cost of not trying it is continuing to burn runway on a model that isn't built for the market we're in.

The market won't wait for us to be ready.
